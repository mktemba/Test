Welcome to Mahjong!

Mahjong is a classic Chinese tile-based game that combines skill, strategy, and a bit of luck. It's typically played by four players and uses a set of 144 tiles.

What You'll Learn

In this interactive tutorial, you'll master the fundamentals of Mahjong:

  • Understanding the different types of tiles
  • How to build winning hands
  • Basic gameplay and rules
  • Strategy and tactics

The goal of Mahjong is to be the first player to complete a winning hand by collecting sets of tiles. A winning hand typically consists of 14 tiles organized into specific combinations.

Did You Know?

Mahjong originated in China during the Qing dynasty and has been played for over 100 years. Today, it's enjoyed by millions of people worldwide!

The Tiles: Suits

Mahjong uses 144 tiles divided into several categories. Let's start with the three main suits:

The Three Suits

  • Bamboo (Sticks): Numbered 1-9, represented by bamboo sticks
  • Characters (Wan): Numbered 1-9, shown with Chinese characters
  • Dots (Circles): Numbered 1-9, displayed as circles

Each suit has tiles numbered from 1 to 9, and there are four identical copies of each tile in the set.

Honor Tiles

In addition to the three suits, Mahjong has special tiles called Honor Tiles. These are divided into two groups:

Dragons (3 types, 4 of each)

  • Red Dragon (中): Red character meaning "center"
  • Green Dragon (發): Green character meaning "prosperity"
  • White Dragon (白): Often blank or with a border

Important Notes

  • Sequences (Chows) can only be made with suited tiles, not honors
  • Honor tiles can only form Pungs or Kongs (identical sets)
  • A sequence must be consecutive numbers in the SAME suit

The Winning Hand

A standard winning hand in Mahjong consists of 14 tiles organized in this pattern:

Winning Hand Formula

4 Sets + 1 Pair = 14 tiles

  • 4 sets of 3 tiles each (Pungs or Chows) = 12 tiles
  • 1 pair of identical tiles = 2 tiles
  • Total = 14 tiles

Gameplay Basics

Now that you understand tiles and winning hands, let's learn how a game of Mahjong is played:

Game Setup

  • Four players sit around a square table
  • Each player is assigned a wind direction (East, South, West, North)
  • The tiles are shuffled and each player draws 13 tiles to start
  • Tiles remaining form the "wall" to draw from

Turn Sequence

  1. Draw: Take a tile from the wall (now you have 14 tiles)
  2. Evaluate: Look at your hand and decide what to discard
  3. Discard: Place one tile face-up in the center (back to 13 tiles)
  4. Claim: Other players can claim your discard for certain sets

Claiming Discards

  • Pung: If you have 2 matching tiles, you can claim a discard to make a Pung
  • Chow: If you're next in turn, you can claim a discard to complete a sequence
  • Mahjong: If a discard completes your winning hand, call "Mahjong!" immediately

Winning

You can win by:

  • Drawing the tile you need from the wall (self-draw)
  • Claiming a discarded tile that completes your hand

The first player to complete a valid 14-tile hand wins the round!

Advanced: Scoring Basics

In Mahjong, different hands are worth different amounts of points. Let's learn the basics of scoring!

Base Scoring System

Mahjong uses a point system where certain combinations earn you more points:

  • Basic Hand: Just completing 4 sets + 1 pair = Minimum points
  • Concealed Hand: No claimed tiles from others = Double points
  • Self-Draw: Drawing your winning tile yourself = Bonus points
  • All Simples: Only 2-8 tiles (no 1s, 9s, or honors) = More points

Value of Sets

  • Chow (Sequence): No extra points - basic set
  • Pung of Simples (2-8): Low value
  • Pung of Terminals (1 or 9): Medium value
  • Pung of Dragons: Medium-high value
  • Pung of Your Wind: Medium-high value
  • Kong (4 identical): Higher value than Pung

Example Scoring

Simple hand: 1-2-3 Bamboo (Chow) + 5-5-5 Dots (Pung) + 7-8-9 Characters (Chow) + East-East-East (Pung) + Red-Red (Pair)

Points: Base (1) + Pung of Simples (1) + Pung of Dragons/Winds (2) = 4 points minimum

Important Note

Scoring systems vary by region (Hong Kong, Japanese Riichi, American, etc.). This is a simplified version to help you understand the concept!

Advanced: Strategy and Defense

Congratulations on making it to the final lesson! Let's explore winning strategies and defensive play.

Offensive Strategy

  • Flexibility: Keep multiple ways to complete your hand
  • Tile Efficiency: Discard tiles that limit your options
  • Speed vs. Value: Decide if you want a fast, low-scoring win or wait for higher value
  • Watch the Discards: Pay attention to what others discard to know what's available
  • Middle Tiles First: 4-5-6 tiles are more flexible than 1-2-3 or 7-8-9

Defensive Strategy

  • Read the Table: Notice which tiles opponents are collecting
  • Safe Tiles: Discard tiles that have already been discarded by others
  • Dangerous Tiles: Avoid discarding tiles close to what opponents are collecting
  • Fold When Needed: If someone is close to winning, play defensively
  • Terminal Strategy: Late-game, 1s and 9s are usually safer to discard

Advanced Tactics

  • Furiten (Riichi): In some variants, you can't win on a tile you previously discarded
  • Blocking: Hold tiles you think opponents need
  • Tile Counting: Track which tiles have been played (4 of each exist)
  • Position Awareness: Remember turn order affects claiming opportunities
  • Risk Assessment: Balance the risk of dealing into someone's hand vs. improving yours

Example: Reading Discards

If a player discards: 1 Bam, 9 Bam, 1 Dot, East Wind

Analysis: They're likely building middle tiles in suits and don't need honors. Be careful discarding 3-7 range tiles in Bamboo and Dots!

Final Tips

  • Practice makes perfect - the more you play, the better you'll recognize patterns
  • Start with simpler hands and work your way up to special hands
  • Learn from every game - even losses teach valuable lessons
  • Play with others to learn different strategies and regional rules
  • Most importantly - have fun! Mahjong is a social game meant to be enjoyed
